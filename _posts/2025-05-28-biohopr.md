title: Paper accepted by ACL 2025 findings – *BioHopR - A Benchmark for Multi-Hop, Multi-Answer Reasoning in Biomedicine* – at [https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.22240](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.22240) <br/><br/>This paper introduces BioHopR, a benchmark for evaluating multi-hop, multi-answer reasoning in the biomedical domain. Built on the PrimeKG knowledge graph, BioHopR captures the complexity of real-world biomedical queries through one-tomany and many-to-many relationships, rigorously assessing reasoning over 1-hop and 2-hop tasks. Evaluation results highlight that O3-mini, a proprietary model with a reasoning step, outperforms open-source models including biomedical models like HuatuoGPT-o1. Across all models, the performance drop from 1-hop to 2-hop tasks underscores the difficulty of aligning intermediate reasoning steps, especially in bridging entities. <br/><br/>Read the full paper at [https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.22240](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.22240).
