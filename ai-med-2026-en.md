## 📌 Course Overview

- Host institution: University of Glasgow
- Course name: Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare: Principles and Application
- Credit: 10 SCQF (Scottish Qualifications and Credit Framework, recognized and transferable in the rest of the UK and some international institutions)
- Duration: 2 weeks
- Aimed at: Global students, no programming background required

---

## ✨ What will you get?

### 🧠 AI Foundation Awareness

- Core AI concepts
- Healthcare scenarios
- Medical data characteristics and challenges

### 🤖 Core machine learning methods

- Decision Tree
- Logistic regression
- Support Vector Machine (SVM)
- Neural Networks / Deep Learning
- Integrated Learning Models

### 🔬 Frontier exploration of AI in healthcare

- Clinical Natural Language Processing (Clinical NLP)
- Generative AI in the biomedical field
- Deep Modeling for Medical Imaging
- AI-assisted clinical decision making

### ⚠️ Limitations and Challenges of Medical AI

- Data bias and fairness issues
- Lack of interpretability
- Deployment risks
- Medical Ethics and Regulation

## ✅ Course Benefits

- Combine theory (AI / Machine Learning / Deep Learning) with real-world medical / biomedical case studies - to help understand the application and challenges of AI in real medical scenarios.
- Collaboration with experienced faculty - Faculty members have AI research background (Prof. Wu) as well as clinical experience and primary care background (Dr. Jani), which is conducive to the understanding of healthcare AI from a multi-dimensional perspective.
- Short-term (two weeks) intensive learning + credits - very friendly for those who want to try out the healthcare + AI intersection but have limited time.
